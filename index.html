About Me

Hello! My name is Morgan Thomas-Price. I am a student at Plymouth University studying Digital Media Design. This is a portfolio containing pieces of my work and links to my social medias. In college I studied Graphic Design, 3D Design and Creative Media. These courses taught me to use softwares like Photoshop to a professional standard and during my studies at university I have learned how to use softwares such as Audacity, Adobe Premier Pro, Atom, Max MSP and some hardwares such as electronic making with Arduino. I am very enthusiastic about technology and love spending my time expanding my horizons by learning by doing within softwares.

Petrol Station Project

This was a project I did in college for 3D Design we were tasked to design a petrol station in Google Sketchup and when the design was complete take the finished model and render it in Photoshop, adding textures, weather effects and people or cars to the image anything to make it seem like it was real. I added several effects including a cityscape in the background and several rain effects including an effect which makes it look like rain has landed on the lens of the camera the photo is being taken on. I really enjoyed this project as it allowed me to be creative in how I rendered my design allowing me to think of various ways to design around my petrol station and I was very happy with the final outcome.

Lego Project

This was a project I did in college for 3D Design we were tasked to create a model using lego and then take photos of the model in many different compositions and then take the photos and edit the models in the landscape photo. I really loved this project because it allowed me to create something, albeit with lego, and then create something with photoshop. It also allowed me to learn new things that I could do within Photoshop for example in this specific example I have supplied the tree in the foreground is in the fact the same tree that is in the background I just used tools in photoshop to cut out the tree and I am really pleased with how it came out as you can barely tell it is the same tree.

Coffee Shop Logo Project

This was a project I did in college for Graphic Design we were tasked to create a logo for a coffee shop. I created several different variations but ended up deciding upon this variation. After we needed to create several examples of the logo in use I created several examples including 2 different styles of the menu, the shop exterior and interior, as well as condiments such as sugar packets, cups and uniforms in aprons. I really enjoyed this project as it had a heavy research side of it as logos are known for being iconic it was important I understood what made certain logos so popular so I could hope to replicate it with my own. I was really happy with my logo as i think it fits the coffee shop aesthetic really well with its stylish font and the overlaid texture.

Typography Project

This was a project I did in college for Graphic Design we were tasked to create several posters based around fonts of our choice. The poster needed to include a small amount of research into the fonts creation and popularisation as well as be visually pleasing. This is one of my early projects at college so its quality isn’t as high as my work is now but I still enjoyed the project very much as it allowed for me to play around in photoshop and create some very good pieces of work the examples I have provided are my poster for the font Baskerville, which was my final outcome for this project as well as my poster for the iconic font Helvetica. I like these two in particular as they each make use of several different photoshop practices. The Helvetica design uses a filter from the filter gallery to give the gradient a more cartoonish look and the Baskerville design uses a 2D texture which using the blending options has been applied to the poster to make the colour seem deeper giving it a more aesthetically pleasing look.
